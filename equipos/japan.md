## Summary

Japan arrive at their 8th World Cup no longer as a dark horse but as a genuine knockout-round contender. Coached by Hajime Moriyasu, they were the first non-host nation to qualify, scoring 54 goals and conceding just 3 across 16 qualifiers, and have beaten England and Scotland on the road in 2026 (plus Germany, Brazil and Spain since 2022). The big question is whether a deep, mostly Europe-based squad can finally break a four-tournament Round-of-16 curse — especially now that talisman Kaoru Mitoma is out injured.

## Tactics

Moriyasu deploys a **three-at-the-back** system (3-4-2-1 / 3-4-3), pragmatic by nature: unleash the attacking talent when possible, sit in a low block when needed.

- **Likely XI:** Suzuki; Taniguchi, Itakura, Ito; Doan, Sano, Kamada, Nakamura; Kubo, Ueda, Maeda
- Identity built on **high pressing and intelligent collective movement** rather than star power.
- Most dangerous in **transition** — rapid wide runners immediately after winning the ball in the middle third.
- Endo's deep positioning is the pivot that keeps the press sustainable.

---

## Strengths

- Mostly elite, Europe-based squad with real top-level pedigree (wins over England, Germany, Spain, Brazil).
- Strong, organized defensive spine and recovery speed from the back three.
- Coordinated, sustainable high press and lethal counter-attacking transitions.
- Creative midfield depth (Kubo, Kamada, Doan) and excellent set-piece/wide delivery.

## Weaknesses

- **No elite out-and-out striker** — tends to create more chances than it converts.
- **Loss of Mitoma** removes the squad's only proven Premier League match-winner on the wing.
- Wing-back system demands huge physical output; flank fatigue is a risk over a long tournament.
- Psychological burden of **four straight Round-of-16 exits** — unproven over a deep run.

---

## Verdict

Japan are a serious threat to anyone in Group F and a likely knockout qualifier — most projections have them advancing (often as runners-up behind the Netherlands), with an outside path to the quarterfinals. The ceiling is capped by the absence of Mitoma and the lack of a top-tier striker, but their pressing system, defensive structure and proven ability to beat elite nations make them one of the most dangerous "mid-tier" sides at the tournament. Realistic expectation: escape the group and finally challenge the Round-of-16 ceiling.
