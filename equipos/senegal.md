## Summary

Senegal arrive as one of Africa's strongest sides: undefeated in qualifying, AFCON 2025 finalists (on the pitch they won the final 1-0 before a controversial post-match walk-off saw CAF overturn the result to a 3-0 Morocco win), and the first African team to beat England at Wembley (3-1, June 2025). Coach Pape Thiaw blends a battle-hardened veteran core — Mané, Koulibaly, Mendy, Gueye — with explosive attacking talent in Jackson, Ismaila Sarr and Iliman Ndiaye. The drawback: a tough group alongside France and Norway, an aging spine, and inconsistent end-product. Realistic ceiling is the quarter-finals.

## Tactics

- **Shape:** 4-3-3, the long-standing Senegal template carried over by Thiaw, with a destroyer-and-runners midfielder and width through Mané/Sarr/Ndiaye.
- **Defensive base:** Compact back line marshaled by Koulibaly with Mendy behind; strong record in qualifying.
- **Transitions:** Pace on both flanks (Sarr, I. Ndiaye, Diao) makes Senegal dangerous on the counter — central to the 3-1 win over England.
- **Midfielder balance:** Gueye anchors, with Pape Matar Sarr / Lamine Camara providing energy and progression.

---

## Strengths

- Undefeated, defensively solid qualifying campaign.
- Elite top-level experience — large core in Europe's top five leagues plus the Saudi Pro League.
- Tournament pedigree: AFCON 2021 winners, AFCON 2025 finalists, beat England at Wembley.
- Genuine pace and physicality in attack and transition.
- Leadership and big-game temperament from Mané, Koulibaly and Mendy.

## Weaknesses

- Aging spine: Mané (34), Koulibaly (35) — questions over legs across a long tournament.
- Inconsistent final-third end product; finishing can desert them in tight games.
- Coach Pape Thiaw's composure under pressure questioned after the AFCON final walk-off fiasco.
- Brutal group draw: France and Norway both stand between them and an easy passage.

## Verdict

A balanced, experienced and physically gifted side that should compete for a top-two finish in Group I and reach the knockouts, though France are clear group favorites and Norway are a live threat. Senegal's pace, defensive structure and big-tournament nous make them a dangerous out, but an aging core and patchy finishing likely cap their ceiling around the **quarter-finals**. At ~125/1 to lift the trophy they are firm outsiders, but a deep run is well within reach.
