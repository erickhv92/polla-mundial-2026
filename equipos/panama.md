## Summary / Verdict

Panama arrive at their second World Cup as a battle-tested, experienced side that topped its CONCACAF qualifying group with room to spare and was unbeaten in its last 12 competitive matches of 2025. Under Thomas Christiansen they are organized, defensively compact and dangerous on transition, built around a veteran spine and one of CONCACAF's best midfielders in Adalberto Carrasquilla. Drawn into a brutal Group L alongside England, Croatia and Ghana, they are clear underdogs (2/1 to advance, 2/5 to be eliminated in the group), but their cohesion and tournament nous make them a genuine threat to steal a result and chase a third-place qualifying spot.

---

## Squad / Key Players

**Captain:** Aníbal Godoy (most-capped ever, 157 caps). **Squad average age ~30** — experience emphasized over youth.

### Star / Key Players

- **Adalberto Carrasquilla** — MF, UNAM (Pumas, Mexico). The creative engine and tempo-setter; widely rated among CONCACAF's best midfielders. Controls progression and build-up. (Minor groin concern noted pre-tournament.)
- **Aníbal Godoy** — MF (captain), San Diego FC (MLS). National-record 157 caps; the experienced defensive anchor and leader.
- **Amir Murillo** — DF (RB), Beşiktaş (Turkey). European-tested right-back, key attacking outlet on the flank.
- **José Córdoba** — DF (CB), Norwich City (England). Young (25) centre-back with European pedigree; anchors the back line.
- **Ismael Díaz** — FW, León (Mexico). Dynamic forward and a primary creative/attacking threat; standout at the 2025 Gold Cup.
- **José Fajardo** — FW, Universidad Católica (Chile). Primary penalty-box scoring threat; netted vital qualifying goals.
- **Carlos Harvey** — MF, Minnesota United (MLS). Energetic box-to-box midfielder providing legs alongside Godoy.
- **Alberto Quintero** — MF/Winger, Plaza Amador. Veteran (38), at his first World Cup after missing 2018 through injury; squad leadership.

### Goalkeepers
Luis Mejía (Nacional), Orlando Mosquera (Al-Fayha — often the qualifying/Gold Cup #1), César Samudio (Marathón).

## Tactics

Christiansen favors a **ball-dominant, positional-play approach**, deploying a fluid **3-4-3 that morphs into a 4-3-3 / 4-5-1** out of possession. The team prioritizes structural security: a compact back four (or three) that slides quickly to deny space, then springs forward on transition through Carrasquilla and the wide forwards. Expect Panama to sit deeper and more reactive against England and Croatia, looking to frustrate, stay compact and counter, while being more proactive against Ghana.

---

## Strengths

- Strong defensive organization and compactness; hard to break down.
- Tournament experience and cohesion — veteran spine, unbeaten run of 12 competitive games in 2025.
- Elite midfield creativity in Carrasquilla; leadership from Godoy.
- European/MLS-based core (Murillo, Córdoba, Harvey, Díaz, Fajardo) used to high-level competition.
- Dangerous on transition and set pieces.

## Weaknesses

- Aging squad (avg ~30, several key players 32–38) — risk of fatigue over a long tournament.
- Limited elite top-end talent compared to England, Croatia.
- Can be passive and pinned back against superior possession sides.
- Thin margin for error in the toughest group draw; goalscoring depth a question.
- Carrasquilla's fitness (groin) is a key dependency — heavy reliance on one creator.
