| | |
|---|---|

| **Group** | G — alongside Belgium (#9), Iran (#21), Egypt (#29) |
| **FIFA Ranking** | 85th (lowest-ranked team at the tournament) |
| **Head Coach** | Darren Bazeley (England) — in charge since 2023 |
| **Captain** | Chris Wood |
| **Likely Formation** | 3-4-2-1 / 5-3-2 (pragmatic, defensive) |

| **Qualified Via** | OFC qualifying — beat New Caledonia 3-0 at Eden Park (Mar 2025); first direct Oceania berth |

---

## Summary

New Zealand return to the World Cup for the first time in 16 years (last appeared 2010), qualifying directly through Oceania for the first time after the OFC was granted an automatic slot in the expanded 48-team format. They are the lowest-ranked side in the tournament and are widely regarded as having one of the weakest squads. Built around all-time top scorer and captain Chris Wood, the All Whites are a physical, organised, counter-attacking team whose realistic ambition is a first-ever World Cup win — most plausibly against Iran — and an outside shot at the expanded knockout rounds.

### Star / Key Players

- **Chris Wood** — Forward, Nottingham Forest. Captain, all-time top scorer and joint record cap-holder. Scored 9 in qualifying (incl. back-to-back hat-tricks vs Samoa and Fiji). Fitness is the major concern: had left knee surgery in Dec 2025 and only returned to scoring in March 2026 after five months out.
- **Liberato Cacace** — Left-back/wing-back, Wrexham. Attacking outlet down the left, key to transitions; one of the squad's most technically gifted players.
- **Marko Stamenic** — Central midfielder, Swansea City. Engine of the midfield, ball progression and energy.
- **Tyler Bindon** — Centre-back, Nottingham Forest. Young, athletic defender; anchors the back line alongside veterans.
- **Sarpreet Singh** — Attacking midfielder, Wellington Phoenix. Creativity and link play behind Wood.
- **Joe Bell** — Midfielder, Viking (Norway). Reliable two-way midfielder controlling tempo.
- **Tommy Smith** — Defender, Braintree Town. Veteran and one of only two survivors from the 2010 World Cup squad; leadership.
- **Max Crocombe** — Goalkeeper, Millwall. First-choice keeper, important given the team's defend-and-counter approach.

## Tactics

Bazeley sets up pragmatically, typically in a three-at-the-back system (3-4-2-1 / 5-3-2) that becomes a compact low-to-mid block out of possession. The plan is to stay organised, defend in numbers, use the wing-backs (Cacace especially) to spring forward, and feed long/direct service to Chris Wood, who holds the ball up and attacks crosses with his aerial strength. Set pieces are a deliberate weapon given the squad's physicality.

---

## Strengths

- Aerial power and physicality, headlined by Chris Wood as a genuine Premier League-quality target man.
- Team organisation, discipline and defensive structure under Bazeley.
- Set-piece threat from a tall, physical squad.
- Experience and leadership spine (Wood, Smith, Boxall, Rufer).
- Tournament motivation — first World Cup in 16 years and chasing a historic first WC win.

---

## Weaknesses

- Lowest-ranked team in the tournament (85th); thin squad depth and limited top-flight quality outside a handful of players.
- Heavy reliance on Chris Wood — and his fitness is uncertain after knee surgery.
- Lack of creativity and chance-creation in open play.
- Defensive lapses under sustained pressure; conceded soft goals in pre-tournament friendlies.
- Untested level of opposition: dominant in weak OFC qualifying, far less battle-hardened than group rivals.

---

## Recent Form (last ~5)

Inconsistent and concerning heading into the tournament. Across the last five they took roughly 1 win, 1 draw, 3 losses (8 scored, 7 conceded):

- **L** Haiti 4-0 NZ (3 Jun 2026, friendly) — heavy defeat in their final tune-up.
- **W** NZ 4-1 Chile — standout result, the attack clicked.
- **L** Finland 2-0 NZ — flat performance.
- **D** NZ 1-1 Norway — competitive draw vs strong opposition.
- **L** Ecuador 2-0 / Colombia 2-1 NZ — back-to-back losses to South American sides.

Trend: capable of a big result on their day, but defensively fragile and inconsistent; the 4-0 loss to Haiti just before the World Cup is a red flag.

---

## Key Injuries / Absences

- **Chris Wood (fitness watch)** — left knee surgery in December 2025; did not play between October 2025 and March 2026. Returned and scoring, but match sharpness and durability are a live concern for the captain and talisman.
- No major outright omissions reported; squad is more limited by overall quality/depth than by absentees.

---

## Verdict

New Zealand arrive as clear Group G underdogs and tournament outsiders (~1500/1). With Belgium and a strong Egypt likely ahead of them, their realistic target is to be competitive, nick a result against Iran, and possibly sneak into the expanded knockout bracket as one of the best third-placed teams (priced around 11/8 to advance). Everything hinges on Chris Wood's fitness and the defence holding firm. Expect a disciplined, physical, low-block side that will be hard to break down but short of firepower — a historic first World Cup win is the bar for success.
