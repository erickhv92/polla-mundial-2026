## Summary

Haiti reach only their second-ever World Cup, and first in 52 years (last: West Germany 1974), as the only Caribbean nation ever to qualify, having topped the CONCACAF final-round group by beating Nicaragua 2-0 on the last matchday — remarkably, after being unable to play "home" qualifiers at home due to the security crisis (matches were hosted in Curaçao). This is a young (average age 24), almost entirely diaspora-built squad coached by Frenchman Sébastien Migné, set up as a disciplined, athletic counter-attacking side. They are the rank outsiders in a brutal group but carry a genuine "spoiler" profile through transition pace and a recently committed Premier League striker in Wilson Isidor.

## Squad & Key Players

The 26-man squad has 3 GKs, 8 defenders, 7 midfielders and 8 forwards. Only one domestic-based player (Woodensky Pierre of Violette AC); the rest developed in France, Belgium, England, Portugal, the US and Canada.

### Star / Key Players

- **Wilson Isidor** — Forward, Sunderland (Premier League). The headline talent. Committed to Haiti in March 2026 after a fan-driven push, choosing Les Grenadiers over France. Scored 6 PL goals in 2025/26 (Sunderland's 2nd-top scorer on their PL return). The team's primary transition weapon — genuine pace and a finisher's instinct.
- **Jean-Ricner Bellegarde** — Midfielder, Wolverhampton Wanderers (Premier League). 27, France-born, committed Aug 2025; has started every match since his debut in qualifying. The most technically gifted midfielder, brings top-flight quality and ball progression.
- **Duckens Nazon** — Forward, Esteghlal (Iran). All-time leading scorer with 44 goals in ~76 caps. 32, a well-travelled (13 clubs, 8 countries) experienced leader. Still the most reliable finisher but past his physical peak.
- **Frantzdy Pierrot** — Forward, Çaykur Rizespor (Turkey, on loan from AEK Athens). Big, physical target man with 32 goals in 49 caps. Provides aerial presence and a focal point.
- **Johny Placide** — Goalkeeper & captain, Bastia. Veteran leader of a young group.
- **Hannes Delcroix** — Defender, FC Lugano. Belgian-developed centre-back, key to the back line's organisation.
- **Carlens Arcus** — Defender (full-back), Angers (France). Provides width and athleticism on the flank.
- **Derrick Etienne Jr.** — Forward/winger, Toronto FC (MLS). Experienced wide attacker offering directness.
- **Louicius Deedson** — Forward/winger, FC Dallas (MLS). Pace and width in transition.

## Tactics

Migné sets Haiti up to defend in a compact 4-4-2 / mid-low block and spring forward fast on turnovers — "defensive organization, athletic transition play" with direct vertical progression. In possession it shapes into a 4-2-3-1 with Bellegarde dictating, wide runners (Deedson/Providence) stretching the defence, and Isidor as the spearhead running in behind. The whole game model is built around game-state management: stay compact, frustrate, and hit on the break with pace.

---

## Strengths

- Fast vertical releases and direct counter-attacking after winning the ball
- Genuine pace and a real top-flight finisher in Isidor as a transition threat
- Compact, disciplined defensive structure and clear tactical identity
- Premier League quality in Isidor and Bellegarde lifting the ceiling
- Youthful, athletic squad with strong attacking depth (8 forwards) and "spoiler" mentality

## Weaknesses

- Poor ball retention after winning possession in their own half; frequent first-pass turnovers
- Vulnerable to sustained pressure — risk of being pinned deep by possession-heavy elite sides
- Reliance on counter-attacks means little plan B when forced to chase a game
- Ageing front-line leaders (Nazon 32, Pierrot) past their physical peak
- Newly assembled diaspora group with limited time playing together; thin elite experience beyond two PL players

## Verdict

Haiti are the longest-priced team in Group C and clear bottom seeds against Brazil, Morocco and Scotland, but they are not a soft touch. With a coherent counter-attacking identity, two Premier League regulars (Isidor, Bellegarde) and real pace in transition, they have a plausible path to nicking a result — most realistically against Scotland — and playing spoiler. Realistic expectation: group-stage exit, but capable of one upset or a hard-fought draw if their defensive block holds and Isidor gets space to run.
