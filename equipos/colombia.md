## Summary

Colombia arrive in North America as one of the tournament's most dangerous dark horses, having reached the 2024 Copa América final (lost to Argentina in extra time) and built an unbeaten run under Néstor Lorenzo that included wins over Germany, Brazil and Spain. With James Rodríguez orchestrating and a peak-form Luis Díaz on the left, they have the creative ceiling to trouble any side — but recent results show that defensive lapses and set-piece fragility remain the cracks elite teams can exploit.

## Tactics

- **Base shape:** Lorenzo's preferred 4-2-3-1, with a double pivot (Ríos + Lerma) shielding the back line behind James, Díaz (left), Arias (right) and Suárez up top.
- **In possession:** Morphs into a back three — right-back Muñoz advances, the left-back inverts. Midfielders control tempo and draw markers to open half-spaces for Díaz to attack.
- **Out of possession:** Sits in a mid-block to frustrate opponents, then springs blistering vertical counterattacks via physical aggression and rapid transitions.
- **Identity:** A hybrid of free-flowing Colombian flair and pragmatic, street-wise Argentine pragmatism.

---

## Strengths

- Elite attacking creativity: James (orchestration) + Díaz (peak club form) is a top-tier creative axis.
- Devastating in transition — turns defensive stops into fast counters.
- Big-game pedigree: 2024 Copa América finalists; scalps over Germany, Brazil and Spain in Lorenzo's tenure.
- Strong central-midfield ball recovery when the pivot is on song.
- Deep, experienced spine playing at top European/South American clubs.

---

## Weaknesses

- Aggressive, body-committing posture leaves defensive shape exposed when caught upfield.
- Set-piece organization wobbles — collapsed in the March 2026 window.
- Individual errors at the back can compound under pressure from elite sides.
- Reliance on James staying sharp and fit; less fluid when he fades.

## Verdict

Colombia are a genuine dark horse with the firepower to reach the latter stages. In a Group K where Portugal are favorites, they should comfortably advance (likely as group winners or runners-up) given DR Congo and Uzbekistan. The ceiling is a quarterfinal or deeper run if James and Díaz click and the defense holds; the floor is an early exit if set-piece and transitional defending betray them against elite opposition. Worth backing as a value outsider at ~33/1.
