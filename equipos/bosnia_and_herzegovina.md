## Summary / Verdict

Bosnia and Herzegovina return to the World Cup for only the second time in their history (first since 2014) after a dramatic qualifying campaign that climaxed with a penalty shootout win over Italy in the playoff final — eliminating the four-time world champions and sending them to a third straight missed World Cup. Coached by former captain Sergej Barbarez, this is a gritty, physical, transition-based side built around 40-year-old talisman Edin Džeko and a rising generation of attackers. They are clear underdogs and the lowest-ranked European team in the field, but Group B is one of the most open in the tournament, and Bosnia's resilience and front-line quality make them a live threat for the second qualification spot behind favourites Switzerland.

**Verdict:** Realistic ceiling is qualification from the group (priced around 5/2 to advance). Expect them to fight Canada for second place. A dangerous, never-say-die outsider rather than a contender — capable of being one of the tournament's surprise packages, but thin in depth and vulnerable at the back.

### Star / Key Players

- **Edin Džeko** — Forward, Schalke 04. Captain and talisman at 40. The last survivor of Bosnia's 2014 World Cup golden generation (Pjanić retired Dec 2025). Still the focal point of the attack; his header forced the rebound that Tabaković scored to equalise against Italy in the playoff final.
- **Ermedin Demirović** — Forward, VfB Stuttgart. In excellent form: 12 goals, 3 assists in the 2025/26 Bundesliga (avg rating ~7.1). A mobile, two-way striker and the most in-form attacker in the squad.
- **Haris Tabaković** — Forward, Borussia Mönchengladbach. Clutch finisher; scored the decisive late equaliser against Italy. Strong aerial presence supporting Džeko.
- **Esmir Bajraktarević** — Attacking midfielder/winger, PSV Eindhoven. One of the brightest young talents; won the Eredivisie title in his debut season at PSV. Key creative outlet and future of the team.
- **Amar Dedić** — Right-back, Benfica. Energetic, attacking full-back at a Champions League club; provides width and forward drive.
- **Sead Kolašinac** — Defender, Atalanta. Experienced, physical leader at the back, providing know-how at a top Serie A side.
- **Nikola Vasilj** — Goalkeeper, St. Pauli. Regular Bundesliga starter; the established No. 1.
- **Kerim Alajbegović** — Forward, RB Salzburg. Promising young attacker adding depth and pace off the bench.

### Notable Absence / Context

- **Miralem Pjanić** retired from international football in December 2025, removing the last creative midfield link to the 2014 generation and placing more creative burden on Bajraktarević and the front line.
- No major late injury withdrawals reported at squad announcement, but the roster skews thin in central midfield depth and at centre-back quality.

## Tactics

- **Formation:** Typically a **4-2-3-1**, with Džeko or Demirović leading the line and Bajraktarević operating in the No. 10 / wide creative role.
- **Style under Barbarez:** Physical, high-energy and built on quick transitions. Bosnia look to defend in a compact mid-block, win the ball and break fast into space, leaning on the aerial and hold-up quality of their forwards.
- A double pivot (e.g. Hadžiahmetović / Šunjić / Gigović) provides defensive screening, freeing the full-backs (Dedić, Kolašinac) to push forward.
- Set pieces and crosses to Džeko/Tabaković are a deliberate weapon.

---

## Strengths

- **Front-line quality and depth** — Demirović (in red-hot Bundesliga form), Tabaković and veteran Džeko give genuine goal threat; supported by young talents Bajraktarević and Alajbegović.
- **Mental resilience / clutch character** — Repeatedly came from behind in qualifying and held nerve in two penalty shootouts (Wales, Italy) to reach the finals.
- **Physicality and transition speed** — Barbarez's high-energy system makes them awkward, direct opponents.
- **Aerial threat** — Strong in both boxes from crosses and set pieces.
- **Experienced spine** — Džeko and Kolašinac add big-game know-how.

## Weaknesses

- **Lowest-ranked European side** — Limited overall squad depth and quality versus tournament-level opposition.
- **Defensive fragility** — Conceded first in several recent matches; centre-back depth is a concern against quick, technical forwards.
- **Age of talisman** — Heavy reliance on a 40-year-old Džeko for leadership and focal-point play.
- **Loss of Pjanić** — Thin on elite creative midfield control to dictate tempo.
- **Inexperience at this level** — Most of the squad has never played at a World Cup.
