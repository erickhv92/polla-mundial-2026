# DR Congo (DR Congo / "Les Léopards") — Scouting Report

**Tournament:** FIFA World Cup 2026 (USA / Canada / Mexico)
**Group:** K — alongside Portugal, Colombia, Uzbekistan
**FIFA Ranking:** 46th (June 2026)
**Confederation:** CAF (Africa)
**Head Coach:** Sébastien Desabre (France) — in charge since August 2022
**Title Odds (to win the World Cup):** ~ +100000 (≈ 1000/1) — extreme longshot
**Captain / Leader:** Chancel Mbemba (CB, Lille)

---

## Summary / Verdict

DR Congo return to the World Cup for the first time since 1974 — when they competed as Zaire — under organised, pragmatic French coach Sébastien Desabre. They qualified the hard way: second behind Senegal in qualifying Group B, then beating Cameroon and Nigeria in the CAF play-off round, before a 1-0 extra-time win over Jamaica in the inter-confederation play-off. The Leopards are defensively excellent, physically powerful, and packed with Premier League and top-five-league experience, but they land in arguably the toughest group at the tournament. Realistic ambition is to spring a surprise and steal second place behind Portugal — but Colombia and Uzbekistan make even the round of 32 a genuine fight.

## Tactics

- **Likely formation:** 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1, flexible to a back-three with wing-backs against stronger opponents.
- **Identity:** Defend deep and compact, stay organised, and punish on the transition with the pace of Wissa/Mayele and overlapping full-backs.
- **Build-up:** Pragmatic rather than possession-dominant; willing to cede the ball to elite sides (Portugal, Colombia) and counter.
- **Set pieces:** A genuine weapon given the aerial size of Mbemba and the defensive unit, plus Kakuta's delivery.
- Desabre's hallmark is structure and discipline — DR Congo are hard to break down and rarely beaten heavily.

---

## Strengths

- Elite defensive organisation — four clean sheets in their last five matches; only one defeat in their last ten.
- Premier League / top-five-league experience across the spine (Wan-Bissaka, Mbemba, Wissa, Masuaku, Sadiki).
- Physical, athletic and quick — built to counter-attack and to compete in duels.
- Strong, deep forward options: Wissa, Mayele (CAF Interclub Player of the Year), Bakambu, Banza.
- Tournament-tested mentality after a grinding qualifying campaign (beat Cameroon, Nigeria, Jamaica) and an AFCON knockout run.
- Experienced, stable management under Desabre with a clear game plan.

---

## Weaknesses

- Brutal group draw — Portugal, Colombia and Uzbekistan leave little margin for error.
- Over-reliance on Wissa's fitness and the front line; can struggle to break down compact, organised opponents.
- Limited possession control against elite ball-dominant sides; can be pinned back for long spells.
- Goalkeeping and squad depth in some areas are a step below the group's top sides.
- A history of conceding decisive late goals (eliminated by Algeria in the 119th minute at AFCON 2025).

## Key Injuries / Absences

- **Yoane Wissa (Newcastle United, FW):** Suffered a PCL knee injury in September 2025 on international duty, days after his £55m move to Newcastle; missed most of 2025-26 and all of AFCON 2025. Back in the World Cup squad but match-sharpness and full fitness are the team's biggest question mark.
- General concern: depth behind the first-choice spine; any further injury to Mbemba or the front line would significantly weaken the side.
