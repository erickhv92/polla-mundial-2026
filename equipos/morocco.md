# Morocco (Atlas Lions) — Scouting Report

> **Group:** C (with Brazil, Scotland, Haiti)
> **FIFA Ranking:** 8th (highest-ranked African team at the tournament, as of April 1, 2026)
> **Head Coach:** Mohamed Ouahbi
> **Title Odds:** ~50/1 to 60/1 (shortest-priced African nation)
> **Status:** Reigning AFCON 2025 champions • 2022 World Cup semi-finalists

---

## Summary

Morocco arrive in North America as the highest-ranked African side and a legitimate dark horse, building on their historic 2022 semi-final run and a 2025 Africa Cup of Nations title. Under new coach Mohamed Ouahbi — who only took over in March 2026 after Walid Regragui's resignation — the Atlas Lions blend hardened 2022 veterans (Hakimi, Bounou, Amrabat, Mazraoui) with a golden generation of youth-tournament winners and an emerging, more proactive attacking identity. The big question is whether they can convert genuine talent into knockout success while handling, for the first time, the pressure of being expected to deliver.

## Tactics

- **Likely formation:** 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1 base, flexible.
- **Identity shift:** Moving away from 2022's deep defensive pragmatism toward a more **proactive, attacking** approach — pressing higher and starting sequences further upfield (~46m).
- **Coaching philosophy:** Ouahbi brings the attack-minded style that won the U20 World Cup, but inherits limited prep time (appointed only ~3 months before kickoff).
- **Engine:** Hakimi as right-sided playmaker/overlap (13 chances created in AFCON knockouts), front-foot pressing to win the ball high.

---

## Strengths

- Defensive resilience — conceded the fewest shots per match at AFCON 2025 (5.6).
- High-pressure effectiveness — 52 high turnovers at AFCON, 12 leading directly to shots.
- Elite individual quality at full-back (Hakimi, Mazraoui) and in attack (Brahim Díaz).
- Tournament-tested core plus a deep, hungry, youth-tournament-winning talent pool.
- Tactical versatility — can sit compact or engage high.

---

## Weaknesses

- Creative struggles vs. low blocks (e.g., 72% possession but zero shots on target in a half vs. Tanzania at AFCON).
- New coach with minimal preparation time and unproven at senior World Cup level.
- Managing expectation — first World Cup where they're expected to deliver, not surprise.
- Reliance on Hakimi's fitness; recent hamstring scare is a lingering concern.

## Verdict

Morocco are the strongest African contender and a credible second-favorite to escape Group C behind Brazil — with a real shot at topping it. Their ceiling is another deep run (round of 16 / quarter-final realistic, semi-final the dream), powered by elite full-backs, Brahim Díaz's creativity, and disciplined high pressing. The risks are a rookie senior coach with little prep time, breaking down stubborn low blocks, and the weight of expectation. **A dark horse worth respecting — and a strong value pick to clear the group stage.**
