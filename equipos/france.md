## Summary

France arrive at the 2026 World Cup as the world's top-ranked side and joint-favourites with Spain, carrying arguably the deepest attacking pool in the tournament around captain and talisman Kylian Mbappé and reigning Ballon d'Or winner Ousmane Dembélé. This is Didier Deschamps' farewell tournament, and Les Bleus — runners-up in 2022 and champions in 2018 — are built to go deep again. The main caveats are a defensive injury cloud (notably William Saliba) and a thin, ageing central-midfield core relative to the firepower up top.

## Tactics

Deschamps' France typically set up in a **4-2-3-1** that morphs into a 4-3-3 in possession. A double pivot (Tchouaméni plus one of Kanté / Koné / Zaïre-Emery) screens the back four and frees the front four to attack. Mbappé operates from the left and drifts central, with Dembélé and Olise providing pace and width on the right and a No. 10 (Cherki / Doué) linking play behind a central striker (Thuram or Mateta). The approach is pragmatic and counter-oriented: sit in a compact mid-block, absorb pressure, and strike in transition through elite individual quality. Full-backs (Koundé, the Hernandez brothers, Gusto, Digne) provide overlapping width.

## Strengths

- World-class, tournament-tested attacking depth — Mbappé, Dembélé, Olise, Thuram, Cherki, Barcola, Doué.
- Elite transition and counter-attacking pace; lethal in open space.
- Top-class goalkeeping in Maignan and big-game experience throughout the spine.
- Tactical pragmatism and a winning culture under Deschamps (2018 winners, 2022 finalists).
- Quality and versatility in wide and full-back areas.

## Weaknesses

- Central-defence injury cloud — William Saliba a major fitness doubt; depth and chemistry at CB uncertain.
- Central midfield is thinner and older (Kanté) relative to the attacking riches; creativity from deep can stall.
- Omission of experienced options (Camavinga left out) reduces midfield insurance.
- Can become over-reliant on individual moments from Mbappé/Dembélé when matches are tight.
- Achilles loss of Ekitike removes a striker option.

- 3-1 win vs Azerbaijan (WC qualifying)

**Trend:** Confident and high-scoring heading into the tournament, with notable friendly wins over Brazil and Colombia; the only blemish a draw with Iceland.

## Key Injuries / Absences

- **William Saliba (Arsenal)** — back injury, very doubtful; a significant blow to the central defence if ruled out.
- **Hugo Ekitike (Liverpool)** — ruptured Achilles, ruled out.
- **Eduardo Camavinga (Real Madrid)** — omitted (injury-disrupted, reduced-minutes season).
- **Randal Kolo Muani** — omitted (Mateta preferred).
- Antoine Griezmann not in the squad (squad-building / generational shift).

## Verdict

France are genuine title contenders and joint-favourites: world No. 1, a farewell mission for Deschamps, and the most fearsome attack at the tournament. If Saliba's fitness resolves and the central midfield holds up against elite opposition, Les Bleus have the ceiling to win it all. Expect them to win Group I comfortably and be among the last four. **Projected: deep run, semi-final or final ceiling.**
