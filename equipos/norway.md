## Summary

Norway return to the World Cup for the first time since 1998, riding a golden generation built around the best striker on the planet, Erling Haaland, and one of the game's finest playmakers in captain Martin Ødegaard. They qualified with a flawless record — eight wins from eight, 37 goals scored and just five conceded — topping a group that included Italy, whom they beat 4-1 at the San Siro. The talent is elite, but defensive depth and the fitness of Ødegaard are the question marks that could decide how far this side goes. They are a genuine dark horse drawn into a brutal group with France.

## Tactics

Solbakken sets Norway up in a **4-3-3** that shifts into a **4-2-2-2** in possession, with Ødegaard operating as a No.10 in the pockets between the lines and the wide players stretching the opposition block to open service lanes for Haaland. The approach blends a **direct high press** with possession-based build-up: they invite the opponent's press, circulate the ball, then explode into fast counterattacks. A **double pivot of Berge and Berg** shields the back four. Solbakken obsesses over "distances" — the spacing between lines and players — to enable zonal defending and ensure the ball carrier always has clean passing options.

## Strengths

- **World-class match-winners:** Haaland and Ødegaard give them top-end quality few teams can match.
- **Ruthless qualifying record:** 8/8 wins, 37 goals scored, only 5 conceded; beat Italy 4-1 away.
- **Attacking depth:** Sørloth, Strand Larsen, Nusa and Bobb provide multiple goal threats and tactical flexibility beyond Haaland.
- **Clear identity:** Cohesive, well-drilled system with elite set-piece weapons (Haaland penalties, Ødegaard deliveries).
- **Momentum & confidence:** A generation finally at a World Cup after 28 years, with strong recent warm-up form.

## Weaknesses

- **Ødegaard's fitness:** Multiple injuries this season (incl. knee issues, missed March friendlies). His absence sharply reduces creative output.
- **Defensive uncertainty:** Centre-back stability is a concern (Heggem injury question); the defence has rarely been tested by truly elite attacks.
- **Untested vs the elite:** Qualifying opposition was modest after Italy; limitations could be exposed early against a top side like France.
- **Haaland club form/freshness:** Below-par club scoring run and fatigue after a gruelling season are minor flags.
- **Loss without stars:** A March friendly defeat to the Netherlands (without Haaland and Ødegaard) showed reliance on key men.

## Verdict

Norway are the tournament's most dangerous dark horse: a top-two-club spine (Haaland, Ødegaard) plus genuine attacking depth and a clear tactical identity. In a three-qualify-from-group format they should reach the knockouts, likely finishing second behind France. The ceiling is high — Haaland can win any match single-handedly — but the floor depends on Ødegaard's fitness and a defence that has yet to prove itself against elite firepower. Expect them to advance; a run beyond the round of 16 hinges on staying healthy and avoiding the very best attacks too early.
