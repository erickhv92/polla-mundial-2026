## Summary

Scotland reach their first World Cup since 1998 after a dramatic qualifying campaign capped by a stoppage-time 4-2 win over Denmark. Steve Clarke's side is built on a resilient back three, midfield energy, and the goal threat of Napoli's Scott McTominay, but a cruel late knee injury to Billy Gilmour robs the team of its midfield metronome. Drawn against Brazil and Morocco — the exact pair that eliminated them in 1998 — plus Haiti, Scotland are firm underdogs whose realistic ambition is to escape the group for the first time in their history.

## Tactics

- Steve Clarke's identity is a **three-man defence** (3-5-2 / 3-4-2-1) that morphs into a back five out of possession, using wing-backs Robertson and Tierney/Hickey for width.
- Compact, disciplined, low-to-mid block; happy to cede possession to stronger sides and counter.
- McTominay given license to arrive late in the box from a number-8/10 role — Scotland's main route to goal.
- Set pieces and stoppage-time character are recurring features (multiple late goals in qualifying).
- Ladbrokes-predicted XI for the Haiti opener used a 4-2-3-1: Gordon; Hickey, Hendry, McKenna, Robertson; Ferguson, Christie, McTominay; Gannon-Doak, McGinn; Dykes — so Clarke may flex shape by opponent.

---

## Strengths

- **Team spirit & resilience** — repeatedly scored decisive late goals in qualifying.
- **Defensive organisation** — back-three system is hard to break down for inferior opponents.
- **Elite-level spine** — Robertson, McGinn and McTominay all play/have played Champions League and European-final football.
- **Leadership & experience** — high-cap captain and vice-captain; tournament-tested core.
- **Goal threat from midfield** — McTominay's late runs are a genuine difference-maker.

## Weaknesses

- **Loss of Billy Gilmour** (knee injury, ruled out) removes the side's deepest-lying controller and ball progressor — a serious blow to midfield balance.
- **Limited cutting edge up front** — no prolific, in-form starting striker; reliance on Dykes/Adams/Shankland.
- **Quality gap to the group's heavyweights** — Brazil and Morocco are markedly stronger.
- **Squad depth in attack** is modest, leaning on domestic Premiership players.
- History of falling short — never escaped a World Cup group stage.

---

## Recent Form

- Qualified **automatically** by topping a competitive UEFA group (first WC since 1998).
- Qualifying highlights: 0-0 vs Denmark; 2-0 vs Belarus; **3-1 vs Greece** (Christie, Ferguson, Dykes); a defeat to Greece; then a **4-2 win over ten-man Denmark** with two stoppage-time goals (incl. Tierney) to seal top spot.
- Final warm-up: **4-1 friendly win over Curaçao** (30 May) — but Gilmour injured in the process.
- Trend: **positive momentum and clutch character**, tempered by inconsistency against organised opposition.

## Key Injuries / Absences

- **Billy Gilmour (Napoli)** — OUT. Knee injury vs Curaçao (30 May); Clarke called it "cruel." Replaced by **Tyler Fletcher**. The single biggest blow to the squad.

---

## Verdict

Scotland arrive on a wave of emotion and momentum, but the draw is unforgiving. With Brazil and Morocco overwhelming favourites to take the top two spots, Scotland's path likely runs through beating Haiti and stealing a result against Morocco to chase a best-third-place berth in the expanded 48-team format. The Gilmour injury weakens an already thin midfield, and the lack of a red-hot striker is a concern. Expect a tough, organised, spirited campaign — competitive but most probably a group-stage exit, with progression as the upside surprise. **Outright title hopes are negligible (~250/1).**
