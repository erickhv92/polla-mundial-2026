---

## Summary / Verdict

Iraq return to the World Cup after a 40-year absence, qualifying as the 48th and final team via a dramatic intercontinental playoff win over Bolivia (2-1) on neutral Mexican soil. Under organised, fitness-first Australian coach Graham Arnold, they are a disciplined, counter-attacking underdog built on an "us-against-the-world" mentality, defensive structure, and a growing European-based diaspora core. Drawn into a brutal Group I with France, Senegal and Norway, Iraq are firm outsiders and most pundits expect them to finish bottom — their opener vs Norway is realistically their only shot at points.

## Tactics

Arnold builds Iraq on **organisation and fitness**: a compact mid-block, narrow and disciplined without the ball, then quick, direct transitions through the strikers (Aymen Hussein's aerial presence, Al-Hamadi's running). Iqbal controls tempo and links defence to attack; Al-Ammari is the creative/set-piece hub.

**Predicted XI (4-5-1/4-4-2):** Hassan; Hussein Ali, Zaid Tahseen, Sulaka, Merchas Doski; Youssef Amyn, Iqbal, Al-Ammari, Bayesh; Aymen Hussein, Al-Hamadi.

**Set pieces:** Corners — Al-Ammari, Ali Jasim. Free-kicks & penalties — Al-Ammari, Aymen Hussein. Set pieces are a genuine scoring route given their aerial threat.

---

## Strengths

- Disciplined, compact defensive shape — hard to break down (kept clean records vs UAE & Saudi Arabia in qualifying).
- Experienced core — many players with 70+ caps; collective organisation hard to replicate quickly.
- Strong aerial/physical threat up front (Aymen Hussein) and direct counter-attacking efficiency.
- Resilient, high-pressure mentality — repeatedly delivered in clutch qualifying moments (90+17' penalty vs UAE; playoff win vs Bolivia).
- Growing European-based contingent adds technical quality (Iqbal, Al-Hamadi, Al-Ammari).

## Weaknesses

- Clear individual-quality gap vs elite opponents — limited top-level European representation.
- Vulnerable to possession-heavy, fast-attacking teams who dominate the ball (exactly France/Senegal's profile).
- Center-back depth and quality concerns; struggles protecting leads and occasional fragility under sustained pressure.
- Low scoring potential — flagged as a candidate for lowest-scoring team of the tournament.

## Path / Outlook

The **Norway opener (mid-June)** is Iraq's single realistic chance at points; results vs France and Senegal are expected to be damage-limitation exercises. Arnold has publicly vowed to "shock the world," but consensus has Iraq finishing 4th in Group I. The tournament is best framed as a milestone return and a learning experience for a maturing, well-drilled side.
