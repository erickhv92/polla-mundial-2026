## Summary

Belgium arrive in North America ranked 9th in the world and unbeaten in their last ten matches, having cruised through UEFA qualifying Group J undefeated (P8 W5 D3 L0, 29 scored, 7 conceded). This is widely framed as the **last dance of the golden generation** — De Bruyne, Lukaku, Courtois and Witsel are all in their thirties — now blended with an exciting new wave of pace out wide. Under first-time international boss Rudi Garcia they are strong favourites to win Group G and reach the knockouts, but defensive inexperience and the fitness of their two talismans (De Bruyne, Lukaku) leave a real ceiling on how far they can go.

### Star / Key Players

- **Thibaut Courtois (GK, Real Madrid)** — World-class goalkeeper and the spine of the team. Back in the fold after his rift with the previous management; elite shot-stopper who can win games on his own.
- **Kevin De Bruyne (AM, Napoli)** — Captain and creative engine, wears No. 7. Top scorer in qualifying with 6 goals despite missing time. Recovered from a high-grade thigh tear (out ~128 days from late Oct 2025); fitness and sharpness are the key question.
- **Jérémy Doku (RW/LW, Manchester City)** — The X-factor. Explosive, direct dribbler and the team's primary game-breaker in 1v1 situations. Wears No. 11.
- **Romelu Lukaku (ST, Napoli)** — All-time leading scorer for Belgium, at his 4th World Cup. Major fitness concern: only ~69 minutes and one goal across 2025-26 with recurring hamstring/hip muscle problems. Selected on reputation and the hope five weeks restores fitness.
- **Leandro Trossard (FW, Arsenal)** — Versatile, in-form attacker who can play across the front line; wears No. 10. Reliable contributor and a likely starter given Lukaku's fitness doubts.
- **Amadou Onana (CM, Aston Villa)** — Powerful, athletic midfield anchor who gives the side physical presence and ball-winning.
- **Youri Tielemans (CM, Aston Villa)** — Composed deep-lying playmaker enjoying strong club form at Villa; brings control and range of passing to the double pivot.
- **Dodi Lukebakio (RW, Sevilla)** — Dangerous inverted winger with a strong left foot cutting inside; depth and a different attacking profile.

## Tactics

Garcia favours a **possession-oriented 4-2-3-1** (shifting to 4-3-3) built around a double pivot of Onana and Tielemans that frees De Bruyne to operate in the No. 10 role. The identity is dynamic, pacey wide play — Doku and Lukebakio/Trossard stretching defences and creating overloads — feeding a central striker (Lukaku when fit, otherwise a false-nine/Trossard solution). Full-backs (Castagne, De Cuyper) push high to support the wingers. The approach is proactive with the ball; the trade-off is the space left behind a relatively young and unproven back line.

---

## Strengths

- **Elite goalkeeper** in Courtois — a genuine match-winner and tournament-grade safety net.
- **Top-tier attacking talent and pace out wide** (Doku, Trossard, Lukebakio, De Bruyne) — capable of breaking down anyone.
- **Midfield depth and balance** — Onana, Tielemans, Vanaken, Witsel, Raskin offer control and physicality.
- **Momentum and stability** — unbeaten in 10, sailed through qualifying without defeat.
- **Big-game experience** in the spine (Courtois, De Bruyne, Lukaku, Witsel).

## Weaknesses

- **Young, inexperienced defence** prone to lapses at key moments; lacks an established elite centre-back partnership.
- **Fitness of the two talismans** — both De Bruyne and Lukaku arrive undercooked after long injury layoffs.
- **Over-reliance on Lukaku** for centre-forward goals, with no proven elite alternative striker.
- **"Inferiority complex" against the very best** and a tendency to be slow starters — psychological/historical baggage from past knockout exits.
- **First-time international coach** (Garcia) untested in major tournament knockout pressure.

## Key Injuries / Absences

- **Kevin De Bruyne** — selected, but only recently back from a ~4-month high-grade thigh tear; sharpness/fitness a question.
- **Romelu Lukaku** — selected despite a near-lost club season (recurring hamstring/hip issues, ~69 mins, 1 goal in 2025-26). Garcia banking on five weeks to restore fitness.
- **Notable omissions:** Loïs Openda (Juventus — just 1 goal in 24 Serie A games), Romeo Lavia (Chelsea — persistent injuries), Mika Godts — left out on form/fitness/squad balance.

---

## Verdict

Belgium are clear favourites to top Group G and reach the knockout rounds, with the attacking firepower (Doku, De Bruyne, Trossard) and a world-class keeper (Courtois) to trouble any opponent on their day. But this golden-generation core is aging and injury-clouded — the De Bruyne/Lukaku fitness gamble plus a green defence cap their realistic ceiling around the quarter-finals. A genuine dark horse if the talismans hit form; a slow-starting early exit risk if they don't. Title odds of ~33/1 feel fair.
