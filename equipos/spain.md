## Summary

Spain arrive at the 2026 World Cup as reigning European champions, the world's No. 2 ranked side, and joint-favorites to lift the trophy. Luis de la Fuente has assembled arguably Spain's most talented squad in a generation, blending the elite possession DNA of Barcelona with a faster, more direct attacking edge through Lamine Yamal and Nico Williams. The headline risk is fitness: Yamal is racing to recover from a hamstring injury and an unusually long injury list has dented the depth, but the core remains formidable and the draw (Group H) is kind.

---

## Squad & Key Players

Full 26-man squad announced 25 May 2026. Notably, **zero Real Madrid players** were selected — a historic first reflecting Madrid's difficult season and Carvajal's injury.

**Goalkeepers:** Unai Simón (Athletic Club), David Raya (Arsenal), Joan García (Barcelona)

**Defenders:** Marcos Llorente (Atlético), Pedro Porro (Tottenham), Marc Pubill (Atlético), Aymeric Laporte (Athletic Club), Pau Cubarsí (Barcelona), Marc Cucurella (Chelsea), Alejandro Grimaldo (Bayer Leverkusen)

**Midfielders:** Rodri (Man City), Fabián Ruiz (PSG), Mikel Merino (Arsenal), Martín Zubimendi (Arsenal), Pedri (Barcelona), Gavi (Barcelona), Dani Olmo (Barcelona)

**Forwards:** Yeremy Pino (Crystal Palace), Álex Baena (Atlético), Mikel Oyarzabal (Real Sociedad), Nico Williams (Athletic Club), Ferran Torres (Barcelona), Borja Iglesias (Celta Vigo), Lamine Yamal (Barcelona)

### Star / Key Players

- **Rodri (DM, Manchester City)** — Captain and 2024 Ballon d'Or winner. Back to full fitness after a season-ending injury in 2024-25; the metronome who anchors everything. First name on the team sheet.
- **Lamine Yamal (RW, Barcelona)** — The generational talent. Recovering from an April hamstring injury; likely to miss the opener and possibly the second group game, targeting a return vs Uruguay. When fit, Spain's most dangerous attacker.
- **Pedri (CM, Barcelona)** — The creative heartbeat, world-class press resistance and tempo control. Healthy and central to the build-up.
- **Nico Williams (LW, Athletic Club)** — Explosive, direct dribbler who provides the vertical threat opposite Yamal. Some fitness concerns noted heading in.
- **Mikel Oyarzabal (FW, Real Sociedad)** — Euro 2024 final match-winner; reliable big-game finisher and the likely starting striker.
- **Pau Cubarsí (CB, Barcelona)** — Teenage center-back already a defensive leader; composed in possession.
- **Dani Olmo (AM/FW, Barcelona)** — Versatile attacking midfielder, scored in the decisive qualifier vs Türkiye.
- **Martín Zubimendi (DM, Arsenal)** — Elite single-pivot cover and rotation option alongside/behind Rodri.

---

## Tactics

De la Fuente sets up in a **4-3-3** built on Spain's traditional positional play and possession dominance, but this iteration is faster and more direct than past sides. Rodri sits as the lone pivot with Pedri and a box-to-box runner (Merino/Fabián) ahead. The width and verticality come from the wingers — Yamal and Nico Williams stretching defenses one-on-one — feeding a mobile No. 9 (Oyarzabal/Ferran). Full-backs invert or overlap depending on opponent. Out of possession Spain press high and aggressively to win the ball in advanced areas. Expect heavy rotation through the group stage given the kind draw and injury management.

---

## Strengths

- **Elite midfield control** — Rodri + Pedri is arguably the best central spine at the tournament; dominates possession and tempo.
- **Tournament pedigree & momentum** — Euro 2024 champions; unbeaten in a national-record run of ~31 consecutive matches.
- **Wing threat & verticality** — Yamal and Nico Williams give Spain a direct, match-breaking edge that previous possession-only sides lacked.
- **Depth in key zones** — Multiple top-club options in midfield (Zubimendi, Fabián, Merino, Gavi, Olmo) and goal (Simón, Raya).
- **Defensive solidity** — Conceded just 2 goals across the entire qualifying campaign.

---

## Weaknesses

- **Injury cloud at the worst time** — Yamal (hamstring), questions over Nico Williams' fitness, and Fermín López ruled out.
- **No recognized world-class striker** — Reliant on Oyarzabal/Ferran; lacks a prolific out-and-out No. 9.
- **Right-back uncertainty** — Carvajal's tournament-ending injury removes a decade-long cornerstone; Porro/Llorente/Pubill fill in.
- **Center-back experience** — A youthful CB core (Cubarsí) leaning on Laporte for leadership.
- **Penalty / fine-margins history** — Lost the 2025 Nations League final to Portugal on penalties despite a draw.

---

## Recent Form (last ~5)

Trend: **excellent, unbeaten, but tapering in friendlies.**

- **2-2 vs Türkiye** (WC Qualifier, Nov 2025) — secured top spot, unbeaten qualifying.
- **6-0 vs Türkiye** (Qualifier) — dominant.
- **3-0 vs Bulgaria** (Qualifier) — comfortable.
- **2-2 vs Portugal** (Nations League Final, June 2025) — drew after extra time, **lost on penalties** (only blemish; ended trophy hopes).
- **0-0 vs Egypt** (Friendly, recent) — flat, goalless pre-tournament tune-up.

Spain finished qualifying unbeaten atop Group E with 21 goals scored and only 2 conceded. They also beat France 5-4 in the 2024-25 Nations League semifinal.

---

## Key Injuries / Absences

- **Dani Carvajal (RB, Real Madrid)** — fractured toe, **out of the World Cup**; end of an era at right-back.
- **Lamine Yamal (RW)** — hamstring (left leg, April 22); in the squad but a major doubt for Match 1 (Cape Verde) and possibly Match 2 (Saudi Arabia); targeting return vs Uruguay (June 26).
- **Fermín López (MF, Barcelona)** — broken foot, **out**.
- **Nico Williams (LW)** — fitness concerns flagged heading into the tournament.
- **All Real Madrid players** — none selected (historic first).

---

## Verdict

Spain are a deserving co-favorite: the best midfield in the world, a kind group, deep squad, and the confidence of reigning continental champions. The ceiling is a second World Cup star (first since 2010). The swing factor is Lamine Yamal's hamstring — if he returns to full sharpness by the knockouts, Spain are the team to beat; if the injuries compound and the striker situation bites in tight games, they are vulnerable in fine-margin knockout ties. Expect them to win Group H comfortably and go deep.
