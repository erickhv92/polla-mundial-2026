# Iran (Team Melli) — Scouting Report

**Group:** G (with Belgium, Egypt, New Zealand)
**FIFA Ranking:** 21st (as of June 2026)
**Head Coach:** Amir Ghalenoei
**Title Odds:** 500/1 to win the World Cup
**Group G winner odds:** ~+650 (third favourite behind Belgium and Egypt)

---

## Verdict (Summary)

Iran arrive at their seventh World Cup as one of Asia's most reliable qualifiers, built on a disciplined, pragmatic defensive structure and the star quality of captain-striker Mehdi Taremi. They have never escaped the group stage in six previous attempts — the worst record among the elite AFC nations — but the 48-team format and a deep, experienced squad make 2026 their most realistic shot at history. Progress likely hinges on beating New Zealand and stealing a result against Egypt, with Belgium the clear group favourite.

## Tactics

- **Likely formation:** 4-4-2 or 4-2-3-1 as the base, with the option to shift to 4-3-3 or a two-striker set-up when chasing a game.
- **Style:** Ghalenoei is a pragmatist. Iran are built on defensive control, a compact mid-to-low block, physical midfield work, and direct service into experienced attackers.
- **Game plan:** Territorial containment, minimise central space, then hit fast transitions — leaning heavily on Taremi to convert limited chances.
- **Engine room:** Ezatolahi and Ghoddos manage central areas; full-backs (Rezaeian, Hajsafi/Mohammadi) provide the width.

---

## Strengths

- Elite focal point in Mehdi Taremi — a genuine world-class finisher and penalty taker.
- Organised, disciplined defensive structure; hard to break down when the low block holds.
- Deep pool of tournament-experienced players (multiple veterans of 2018/2022).
- Physicality and aerial presence; can match Egypt and New Zealand bodily.
- Strong set-piece delivery via Jahanbakhsh.

---

## Weaknesses

- Heavy over-reliance on Taremi for goals — limited attacking depth, worsened by the Azmoun omission.
- Pragmatic, reactive approach can leave them passive and toothless against better sides.
- Ageing spine (several key men 32+) raises fitness/legs questions across a long tournament.
- Vulnerable to quality opposition — the 2-1 loss to Nigeria exposed lapses against stronger teams.
- Persistent mental block: zero knockout-stage appearances in six prior World Cups.
