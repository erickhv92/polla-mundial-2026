## Summary

Jordan reach their first-ever World Cup riding the momentum of a historic run to the 2024 AFC Asian Cup final and a commanding qualification campaign (sealed with a 3–0 win away to Oman in June 2025). They are a disciplined, well-organized side built around collective effort and lethal transitions, but they are heavily dependent on European-based star Mousa Al-Tamari for creativity and end product. Drawn into a brutal Group J alongside reigning champions Argentina, plus Algeria and Austria, the realistic ambition is a competitive showing and ideally a maiden World Cup point or win.

## Tactics

- **Shape:** Pragmatic 4-2-3-1 / 4-3-3 under Sellami, prioritizing defensive structure and compactness.
- **Identity:** Collective organization and discipline over individual flair. Two-man midfield pivot (Al-Rashdan + partner) shields the back four.
- **In possession:** Look to control midfield, but most dangerous on quick counters and transitions, releasing Al-Tamari into space.
- **Out of possession:** Can press high in spells; against elite sides they will sit deeper in a low-mid block and absorb pressure.
- **Set pieces:** A genuine weapon given aerial presence of Al-Arab/Haddad and Tamari's delivery.

---

## Strengths

- Tight defensive organization and team discipline — hard to break down.
- A genuine difference-maker in Mousa Al-Tamari (pace, dribbling, transitions, set-piece delivery).
- Strong recent pedigree: 2024 Asian Cup finalists, momentum and belief.
- Dangerous on the counter-attack and from set pieces.
- Settled core that has played together through qualifying — cohesion and continuity.

---

## Weaknesses

- **Over-reliance on Al-Tamari** — limited Plan B if he is marked out or off-form.
- World Cup **debutants** with little experience versus elite opposition; squad is largely domestic/regional-league based.
- Goal-scoring depth thin behind Olwan.
- Weakened spine due to injuries (lost two important regulars — see below).
- Recent defensive lapses in friendlies (conceded heavily to Switzerland).

## Verdict

Jordan arrive as feel-good debutants with a clear identity, a star in Al-Tamari, and recent finalist pedigree — but they were handed arguably the toughest possible draw. Against the world champions Argentina, a physical Algeria and a disciplined Austria, advancing from Group J is a long shot. Realistic success looks like one positive result (a draw or shock win, most plausibly vs Algeria or Austria) and a competitive, organized tournament that announces Jordanian football on the world stage. **Outsiders to progress; playing for history and a maiden World Cup point.**
