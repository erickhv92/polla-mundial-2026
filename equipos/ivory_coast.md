## Summary

Ivory Coast arrive as reigning African champions and one of the form sides of CAF qualifying, having topped their group unbeaten with the best goal difference in African qualification and an extraordinary defensive record (10 clean sheets in 10 games). Coached by AFCON-winning Emerse Faé, the Elephants pair a powerful, experienced midfield spine with electric pace out wide and a deep pool of European-based talent. The challenge is converting that quality into knockout football: they have never advanced beyond the World Cup group stage, and they land in a tough Group E behind favourites Germany, fighting Ecuador for the second qualification spot.

## Tactics

Emerse Faé sets up in a **4-3-3** built on a strong three-man midfield (Kessié, Sangaré, Seri/Fofana) controlling tempo, with width and pace provided by Amad Diallo and Adingra. The side blends possession control with quick, direct transitions exploiting wing speed. Defensively they are extremely well-drilled and compact, the foundation of their clean-sheet streak in qualifying. Faé has flexibility to rotate a deep forward pool (Bonny, Guessand, Wahi, Pépé, Yan Diomande) depending on opponent.

---

## Strengths
- Dominant, experienced midfield spine (Kessié, Sangaré) controlling games.
- Outstanding defensive organization — best defensive record in CAF qualifying (10 clean sheets in 10 matches).
- Electric pace and dribbling on both flanks (Amad Diallo, Adingra).
- Strong squad depth, especially in attack, drawn from top European leagues.
- Winning mentality and momentum as reigning AFCON 2024 champions.
- Proven, tactically astute coach in Emerse Faé.

## Weaknesses
- No history of advancing past the World Cup group stage (qualified 2006/2010/2014, never reached knockouts).
- Questionable elite finishing/centre-forward reliability after omitting Sébastien Haller; goalscoring depends on collective rather than a proven No. 9.
- Squad unproven together at World Cup tournament level.
- Drawn in a difficult group; must beat Ecuador head-to-head and likely take points off Germany to advance.
- Goalkeeping is solid but not star-level.

## Recent Form

**Qualifying (CAF Group F): Unbeaten — topped the group with 8 wins, 2 draws, +25 GD, 10 clean sheets.**

- Sealed direct qualification with a **3-0 home win vs Kenya** in Abidjan.
- Two draws came against Gabon and Kenya during the campaign.
- Overall trend: **strongly positive** — dominant, defensively impeccable, and carrying the confidence of reigning continental champions.

---

## Key Absences / Injuries
- **Sébastien Haller** (former Borussia Dortmund striker) — surprisingly omitted from the squad, the headline absence and a notable loss of a proven centre-forward.

---

## Verdict

Ivory Coast are a genuine dark horse to escape Group E. Their elite midfield control, exceptional defensive structure, and flank pace make them a hard team to beat, and Faé's pragmatism plus AFCON-winning pedigree give them a real edge over Ecuador in the race for second. The lingering doubt is goalscoring without a settled striker and their persistent failure to translate talent into World Cup knockout football. **Realistic ceiling: a Round of 32 / Last 16 berth via second place; first-time progression past the group stage is achievable but far from guaranteed.**
