---

## Summary

Sweden reached the 2026 World Cup via a "great escape": they finished bottom of their qualifying group with just two points and no wins, got Jon Dahl Tomasson sacked, then backed into the UEFA play-offs through their Nations League ranking. Graham Potter took over in October 2025 and steadied the ship in five months, beating Ukraine 3-1 and Poland 3-2 in the play-offs to seal a 13th World Cup appearance and their first since 2018. On paper they own one of the most dangerous attacking front lines at the tournament (Gyökeres + Isak + Elanga), but a leaky defense and serious questions over Isak's fitness make them volatile. Bookmakers expect a third-place group finish at best.

## Tactics

Graham Potter deploys a **3-4-2-1**, using a back three for defensive cover while wing-backs (Gudmundsson, Svensson) provide width and overlap. The system is built to maximize the attacking front line — physical, pressing, direct play funneled through Gyökeres with Isak and Elanga supporting. Potter has worked on defensive structure but organizational frailties persist. Sweden are most dangerous in transition and from the power/movement of their forwards.

---

## Strengths

- **Elite attacking firepower** — a fully fit Gyökeres-Isak partnership is among the best front lines at the tournament.
- **Gyökeres in red-hot form** — Arsenal's leading scorer and the man who single-handedly dragged Sweden through the play-offs.
- **Pace and directness out wide** via Elanga.
- **Big-club spine** — most key players play in the Premier League / top European leagues.
- **Momentum & low pressure** — qualified against the odds, "playing with house money."

## Weaknesses

- **Poor defensive record** — conceded 12 goals across six qualifying games; metrics weak throughout.
- **Isak's fitness** — injury-hit, no full 90 for Liverpool all season; reliability a major question.
- **Key absence: Kulusevski** — creative hub missing entirely (season-long injury).
- **New manager, short runway** — Potter has had only months to instill his system.
- **Tough group** — must navigate Netherlands and Japan to advance.

**Trend:** Sharp upturn under Potter in the play-offs after a dismal qualifying group; attack carrying the team, defense still leaking.

## Key Injuries / Absences

- **Dejan Kulusevski (Tottenham)** — out, season-long injury; misses the World Cup entirely.
- **Alexander Isak (Liverpool)** — fitness doubt; injury-hit season, Potter hopes he's ready by kickoff.
- **Emil Holm** — injured days before the tournament, replaced by **Herman Johansson (FC Dallas)**.

---

## Verdict

Sweden are a boom-or-bust side. If Isak is fit and the Gyökeres-Isak axis fires, they can hurt anyone and steal a Round-of-16 spot. But a fragile defense, a missing creative hub in Kulusevski, and a brutal group (Netherlands, Japan) make group-stage elimination — likely as third place — the bookmakers' base case. Title odds of 100/1 confirm their status as outsiders. Realistic ceiling: Round of 32/16; realistic floor: out at the group stage.
