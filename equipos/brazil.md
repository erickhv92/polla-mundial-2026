## Scouting Summary

Brazil arrive in North America under Carlo Ancelotti, who took over from Dorival Júnior in 2025 and is chasing the nation's first World Cup since 2002. The squad blends elite European attacking talent — Vinícius Jr., Raphinha, Matheus Cunha — with a battle-tested spine of Alisson, Marquinhos, Casemiro and Bruno Guimarães. Ancelotti favours controlled possession and wide isolation to release his wingers, but the side has looked inconsistent in friendlies and is missing two of its brightest young attackers (Rodrygo and Estêvão) to injury. The aging but symbolic inclusion of Neymar adds intrigue and risk.

## Tactics

- **System:** Base 4-3-3, flexing to 4-2-3-1 depending on opponent.
- **Style:** Patient, controlled buildup rather than forced vertical passing. Ancelotti looks to overload and isolate the wingers in high-value 1v1 zones.
- **Attacking plan:** Vinícius isolated on the left, Raphinha for right-side delivery and combinations, with the No.9 (Cunha) linking and finishing. Casemiro provides the platform for Guimarães and the attackers to push high.

---

## Strengths

- Elite, match-winning individual attackers (Vinícius Jr., Raphinha) capable of deciding tight games.
- Experienced, world-class spine: Alisson in goal, Marquinhos in defence, Casemiro/Guimarães in midfield.
- A coach with the deepest big-game / knockout pedigree in world football (5 Champions League titles).
- Strong attacking output recently (6-2 demolition of Panama, 20 shots, 3.37 xG).
- Heavy favourites to top a winnable group, likely securing a softer round-of-32 path.

## Weaknesses

- Chronic inconsistency at international level; star-studded but not always cohesive.
- Structural vulnerability in the space behind advanced full-backs when both wingers push high.
- Unsettled centre-forward position (Cunha vs. Endrick vs. Neymar) — no clear, in-form No.9.
- Neymar gamble: fitness, sharpness and ego/discipline management risks.
- Loss of two dynamic young attackers (Rodrygo, Estêvão) thins the creative depth.

## Verdict

Brazil are clear Group C favourites and should advance comfortably, with the attacking firepower to beat anyone on their day. But the 8/1 title price reflects genuine doubts: an unsettled striker position, defensive exposure when committing both wingers forward, the Neymar gamble, and a habit of underperforming relative to individual talent. With Ancelotti's knockout pedigree they have a deep run in them, yet a quarterfinal exit is the realistic baseline projection unless the front line gels quickly. **Dangerous, talented, but not the safest bet at the top of the market.**
