## Summary

Uzbekistan arrives at its **first-ever World Cup** as a disciplined, defensively-organized side now coached by 2006 World Cup-winning Italian legend Fabio Cannavaro, who took over in October 2025 after qualification was already secured. Built around Manchester City centre-back Abdukodir Khusanov and all-time top scorer/captain Eldor Shomurodov, the team is a heavy underdog in a brutal Group K (Portugal, Colombia, DR Congo) but the expanded 48-team format — where four of six third-placed teams advance — gives them a realistic, if narrow, knockout path.

## Tactics

- **Formation:** Cannavaro favours a **back five with three central midfielders** (5-3-2 / 3-5-2), prioritizing defensive structure above all.
- **Out of possession:** Compact, deep block; concede territory, protect the box, stay disciplined.
- **In possession:** Direct, transition-based football. Width comes from **overlapping wing-backs** and wingers rather than slow build-up.
- **Attacking plan:** Get the ball quickly to Shomurodov up top with Fayzullaev linking behind; rely on set pieces and counters.

---

## Strengths

- **Defensive organization & discipline** — Cannavaro's structured back five is hard to break down.
- **Premier League-level centre-back** in Khusanov (pace + physicality).
- **A genuine goal threat and leader** in captain Shomurodov, in excellent club form.
- **Promising young creative core** (Fayzullaev, Masharipov) with a high ceiling.
- **Solid qualifying pedigree** — defensively miserly route to a debut World Cup.

## Weaknesses

- **Lack of World Cup / elite tournament experience** — debutants on the biggest stage.
- **Limited squad depth** beyond the top names; many players in Iranian, Turkish, Russian and Gulf leagues rather than Europe's elite.
- **Goal-scoring over-reliance on Shomurodov** — thin if he is marked out or injured.
- **Khusanov's match sharpness** after an injury-affected, low-minutes club season.
- **Brutal group draw** — Portugal and Colombia are clearly superior on paper.

## Verdict

Uzbekistan are credible, well-drilled debutants but face arguably one of the toughest group draws. Realistic ambition: be hard to beat, target a result against DR Congo, and sneak through as **one of the best third-placed teams**. With Cannavaro's defensive blueprint, a Premier League defender, and an in-form captain up front, they can frustrate better sides — but a lack of depth and big-stage experience makes advancing a genuine stretch rather than an expectation. **Likely outcome: bottom-half of the group, with an outside shot at the expanded-format knockouts.**
