## Summary

Czechia ended a 20-year World Cup absence by surviving a nerve-shredding playoff path, beating the Republic of Ireland and then Denmark on penalty shootouts under new coach Miroslav Koubek, who took over from Ivan Hašek in December 2025 and will be the oldest manager in tournament history. They are a physical, set-piece-oriented, well-drilled side built around Bayer Leverkusen striker Patrik Schick and a spine of Slavia Prague and Premier League / Bundesliga players. Realistic ambition is to escape a winnable group and reach the Round of 32/16; they are firmly among the tournament's outsiders.

## Squad & Key Players

**Star / Key Players**

- **Patrik Schick** (Forward, Bayer Leverkusen) — Talisman and main goal threat. Outstanding 2025/26: 16 Bundesliga goals (29 in all competitions), including a late-season hot streak. The team's primary route to goals.
- **Pavel Šulc** (Attacking midfielder, Olympique Lyonnais) — Czech Footballer of the Year 2025. Lyon's most decisive attacker (double-digit goals + assists in Ligue 1). The creative engine and a rising star.
- **Ladislav Krejčí** (Centre-back, Wolverhampton Wanderers, on loan from Girona) — Team captain. Ball-playing defender who can also operate in midfield; leads a physically imposing back line.
- **Tomáš Souček** (Midfielder, West Ham United) — Most-capped, most experienced player. Box-to-box presence and a major aerial / set-piece weapon at both ends.
- **Adam Hložek** (Forward, TSG Hoffenheim) — Versatile attacker; his fitness/return from injury is a key prep storyline. Provides pace and a second scoring option.
- **Lukáš Provod** (Midfielder, Slavia Prague) — Technical left-sided creator, important for transitions and dead-ball delivery.
- **Vladimír Coufal** (Right-back, TSG Hoffenheim) — Experienced, combative full-back; energy and crossing down the right.

## Tactics

Koubek favours a pragmatic, organised approach — typically a back four in a 4-2-3-1 (with the flexibility to shift to a back three), prioritising defensive compactness and quick transitions to feed Schick. The side is tall and physically dominant, leaning heavily on set pieces (Souček, Krejčí, Schick all aerial threats) as a reliable scoring source. Šulc and Provod provide the limited creative spark in open play. Expect them to sit a touch deeper against stronger opponents and look to win matches in the air and on the counter.

---

## Strengths

- Elite, in-form focal point in Patrik Schick to convert chances.
- Genuinely dangerous from set pieces — aerial power across the spine.
- Physical, tall, cohesive squad with strong team organisation.
- A breakout creative star in Pavel Šulc (Czech Player of the Year).
- Battle-hardened: survived two playoff penalty shootouts, showing nerve and resilience.
- Solid goalkeeping depth (Staněk, Kovář).

---

## Weaknesses

- Outside Schick and Šulc, limited individual top-tier quality; thin on elite creators.
- Heavy reliance on a domestic (Slavia/Sparta/Plzeň) core that lacks elite-level experience.
- Can be passive and over-reliant on set pieces when forced to break down deep blocks.
- New coach with limited time to embed his system before the tournament.
- Defensive consistency questionable — drew both qualifying playoff legs 2-2 before winning on penalties.
- Hložek's fitness/injury return is a question mark over attacking depth.

## Verdict

Czechia are dark-horse outsiders with a clear identity: defend well, dominate set pieces, and feed a top-class striker in Patrik Schick. Drawn into one of the most open groups at the tournament (co-hosts Mexico aside), advancing from Group A is a realistic target, and in the expanded 48-team format the Round of 32 — even the Round of 16 — is attainable. Beyond that, a lack of elite depth and creativity makes a deep run unlikely. **Projected ceiling: Round of 16. Floor: third place and group exit.**
