## Summary / Verdict

Croatia arrive at their third straight major-tournament knockout-pedigree campaign as a battle-tested, midfield-driven side that punches above its FIFA ranking. They cruised through qualifying (7W-1D, 26 GF / 4 GA — their best-ever qualifying record) and remain dangerous through the timeless Luka Modrić, now 40 and at his sixth World Cup. Their ceiling is a quarter/semi-final run if the spine stays fit; their floor is dragged down by an aging core and a defense that can be exposed in transition. In Group L they are clear second favourites and should advance — likely as runners-up behind England, setting up a kind draw if they avoid defeat in the opener.

## Tactics

- Base shape **4-3-3** that morphs to **4-2-3-1**, built entirely around **midfield control** — Modrić, Kovačić and Pašalić dominate possession, dictate tempo and win the territorial battle.
- Dalić preaches **character, energy and structure**: disciplined out of possession, patient in build-up, lethal on **rapid transitions** with Kramarić attacking the channels.
- Wide players (Perišić) provide **set-piece and crossing delivery**; full-backs push high, which is both a creative outlet and a defensive risk.

---

## Strengths

- Elite, tournament-tested midfield trio (Modrić–Kovačić–Pašalić) that controls games.
- Outstanding qualifying form: 7W-1D, 26 scored, only 4 conceded.
- Deep big-game / knockout experience and mental composure under pressure (shootout pedigree).
- Quality at the back when fit, anchored by Gvardiol.
- Reliable, experienced goalkeeper in Livaković.

## Weaknesses

- **Aging core** — Modrić (40), Perišić (37), Kramarić (34), Budimir (34); legs and recovery pace a concern over a long tournament.
- **Heavy dependence** on a handful of stars (Modrić, Kovačić, Gvardiol) — drop-off when any are missing.
- **Defensive vulnerability in transition** — space behind advancing full-backs can be exploited.
- **Goalscoring reliance** — limited proven elite striker; lean on Kramarić and midfield runners.
