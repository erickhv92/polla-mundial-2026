## Summary / Verdict

Algeria return to the World Cup for the first time since 2014, arriving on the back of a dominant CAF qualifying campaign (8 wins from 10, +16 goal difference) and strong recent form. Led by Vladimir Petković and captained by veteran Riyad Mahrez, this is a technically gifted, European-based squad with genuine quality in wide and attacking areas, but it lands in a tough Group J headlined by reigning champions Argentina. Realistic ceiling is escaping the group — the expanded 48-team format means a third-place finish could be enough — with the knockouts a stretch but not impossible.

### Star / Key Players

- **Riyad Mahrez** — RW/AM, Al-Ahli (KSA). Captain and talisman. Ex-Man City title and Champions League winner, 2019 AFCON hero. Still the team's creative reference point from the right.
- **Amine Gouiri** — ST, Olympique Marseille. In-form Ligue 1 centre-forward; the squad's most reliable modern goal threat.
- **Mohamed Amoura** — FW, VfL Wolfsburg. Pace and directness; a high-ceiling Bundesliga attacker.
- **Rayan Aït-Nouri** — LB, Manchester City. Elite ball-carrying full-back with defensive range and versatility.
- **Ramy Bensebaini** — CB/LB, Borussia Dortmund. Experienced, physical defender with set-piece value; defensive anchor.
- **Ibrahim Maza** — AM/CM, Bayer Leverkusen. Brightest young talent; press-resistant and creative.
- **Nabil Bentaleb** — CM, Lille. Recalled veteran; Petković's preferred deep midfield orchestrator (started over Bennacer).
- **Luca Zidane** — GK, Granada. Son of Zinedine Zidane; notable goalkeeping option.

## Tactics

- **Formation:** Likely **4-3-3**, with flexibility to shift to **4-2-3-1**.
- Petković favours a balanced, possession-capable setup built around technical European-based players.
- Mahrez drifts in from the right to create; Aït-Nouri provides overlapping width and ball progression from left-back.
- Midfield anchored by Bentaleb, with Maza/Aouar/Chaïbi providing creativity between the lines.
- Defensive solidity has been a feature — clean sheets in 5 of their last 7 matches.

---

## Strengths

- Elite attacking talent and wide creativity (Mahrez, Gouiri, Amoura, Aït-Nouri).
- Deep pool of players competing in top European leagues (Premier League, Bundesliga, Ligue 1).
- Excellent recent defensive record and ability to keep clean sheets.
- Strong winning momentum from qualifying (8 wins/10) and good 2025–26 form.
- Tournament pedigree: two-time AFCON champions (1990, 2019); experienced leadership.

---

## Weaknesses

- Brutal group: Argentina are clear favourites, making top-two difficult.
- Some reliance on an aging talisman (Mahrez, now in the Saudi league rather than at elite European level).
- Goalkeeping is unproven at World Cup level (Luca Zidane relatively untested at the very top).
- Questionable depth/finishing consistency if Gouiri or Amoura are unavailable.
- No World Cup experience in the current core — first appearance for the group since 2014.
