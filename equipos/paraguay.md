## Summary / Verdict

Paraguay return to the World Cup for the first time since 2010, ending a 16-year drought thanks to a remarkable turnaround under Gustavo Alfaro. The Argentine coach took over in August 2024 and engineered a near-unbeaten qualifying run, with statement wins over Brazil, Argentina and Uruguay, conceding only ~10 goals across the campaign and keeping seven clean sheets. They are not contenders to win the tournament, but they are a disciplined, hard-to-beat outfit that no one in Group D will relish facing. Realistic ambition: escape the group and cause an upset.

## Tactics

Alfaro favours a robust, highly physical **4-4-2 / 4-3-3 defensive shape** that completely suffocates central spaces. The team is drilled into a disciplined, synchronised mid-to-low block, happily surrendering possession and territory to choke out opposing playmakers, then striking on the counter through Almirón and Enciso. Risk is minimised in their own third, leaning on a dominant centre-back pairing (Gómez–Alderete) to control the box. Set pieces are a genuine weapon given the aerial presence at the back. Alfaro is known for a motivational, psychology-driven man-management style.

---

## Strengths

- Elite defensive organisation — conceded only ~10 goals in qualifying, 7 clean sheets.
- Strong, experienced centre-back pairing (Gómez, Alderete) and aerial dominance.
- Big-game pedigree built in qualifying (beat Brazil, Argentina, Uruguay).
- Counter-attacking threat and individual flair via Enciso and Almirón.
- Tactical discipline and strong team spirit under Alfaro; full squad fit.
- Set-piece danger.

---

## Weaknesses

- Limited sustained goalscoring — reliant on a few moments of quality; can be passive.
- Inconsistent recent friendly form (2 wins in last 5).
- Lacks the depth and individual ceiling of the tournament's top sides.
- Cedes possession by design; can struggle when forced to chase a game.
- Ageing core in key defensive positions.
