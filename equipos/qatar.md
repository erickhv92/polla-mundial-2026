## Summary

Qatar reach their second consecutive World Cup, but for the first time qualified on merit rather than as hosts. Now under former Spain and Real Madrid boss Julen Lopetegui, they bring a near-entirely domestic squad built around two-time AFC Player of the Year Akram Afif and all-time top scorer Almoez Ali. Drawn in a tough Group B, they are widely considered the weakest side and face an uphill battle simply to reach the round of 32.

## Tactics

Lopetegui imposes a possession-based **4-3-3** with a high defensive line and quick combination play through midfield, with a fallback **4-2-3-1** that offers more defensive cover. The system funnels play to Afif on the flanks to create for Almoez Ali through the middle. The high line is a calculated gamble — effective in Asian football but exposed against faster, more clinical opponents.

---

## Strengths

- **Elite Asian attacking duo:** Afif's creativity and Almoez Ali's finishing are genuine continental-class assets.
- **Squad cohesion:** Almost all players share the same domestic clubs (heavy Al-Sadd / Al-Duhail core), giving strong familiarity and chemistry.
- **Experienced spine:** Veteran leaders (Al-Haydos, Khoukhi, Boudiaf) plus the tactical pedigree of a top European coach in Lopetegui.
- **Tournament pedigree:** Reigning-era Asian Cup winners (2019, 2023) with knockout know-how at confederation level.

---

## Weaknesses

- **Lack of elite-level exposure:** Only one Europe-based player; the squad is untested against top-tier opposition week to week.
- **Poor recent form:** Winless and goal-shy in the run-up to the tournament (see below).
- **Defensive fragility:** High line is exploitable; conceded heavily in recent matches.
- **Goal drought:** Just 2 goals in the last 5 games — the attack has misfired against organized defenses.
- **Group difficulty:** All three group rivals are superior on paper.

## Verdict

Qatar are the clear outsiders of Group B and of the tournament at large (~1000/1). Lopetegui's coaching nous and the Afif–Almoez axis give them a puncher's chance in any single game, but wretched recent form, a domestically-confined squad, and three stronger group rivals make even reaching the expanded round of 32 (as a top-eight third-placed team) their realistic ceiling. Expect a competitive but likely short campaign.
