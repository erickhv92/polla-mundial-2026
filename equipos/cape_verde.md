## Scouting Summary

Cape Verde, an Atlantic archipelago of just over 500,000 people, reaches its first-ever World Cup as one of the great stories of the 2026 edition. Coach Bubista has forged a disciplined, defensively organised side built on a diaspora of Portuguese-, Dutch- and US-based professionals, topping a tough African qualifying group ahead of Cameroon. Drawn into a brutal Group H with world No. 2 Spain, Uruguay and Saudi Arabia, they are clear underdogs, but their compact structure, set-piece threat and transition speed make them credible giant-killers on their day.

## Squad & Key Players

The squad is notable for having only one player from Europe's top-five leagues — centre-back Logan Costa of Villarreal. The rest are spread across Portugal, the Netherlands, Cyprus, Turkey, the Gulf, MLS and Eastern Europe.

### Star / Key Players

- **Logan Costa** — CB, Villarreal (Spain). Paris-born, widely regarded as Cape Verde's best player. **Fitness gamble**: ruptured his ACL in pre-season (July 2025) and only returned as a late substitute on 17 May 2026. Match sharpness is a major question.
- **Ryan Mendes** — Winger/Forward (Captain), Iğdır FK (Turkey). All-time leader in caps (94) and goals (22). Veteran leader (36) with European pedigree at Lille, Le Havre and Nottingham Forest.
- **Dailon Livramento** — Forward, Casa Pia (Portugal). Age 24, the team's main attacking reference. Scored decisive qualifying goals (vs Cameroon, opener vs Eswatini); 4 qualifying goals.
- **Jamiro Monteiro** — Midfielder, PEC Zwolle (Netherlands). Engine of midfield, technical quality and goal threat (2 qualifying goals).
- **Roberto Lopes ("Pico")** — CB, Shamrock Rovers (Ireland). Experienced, dependable defensive leader; veteran of the 2022 qualifying campaign.
- **Vozinha** — Goalkeeper, Chaves (Portugal). First-choice keeper, key to the unbeaten/clean-sheet home qualifying record.
- **Garry Rodrigues** — Forward, Apollon Limassol (Cyprus). Technically gifted, big-tournament composure; ex-Galatasaray.
- **Willy Semedo** — Winger, Omonia Nicosia (Cyprus). 2 qualifying goals; pace and directness off the flank.

## Tactics

Bubista deploys a **compact 4-2-3-1** prioritising defensive security and direct, transitional attacking. The double pivot protects central areas and funnels opponents into wide zones where the danger is more manageable. Against the elite sides of Group H, expect a **deep low block (effectively 4-5-1 / 4-3-3 out of possession)**, aggressive ball recovery in useful areas, and quick breaks before opponents reorganise. Set pieces are a genuine weapon. The structure is the star — collective discipline over individual brilliance.

---

## Strengths

- **Defensive organisation** — conceded zero goals at home across qualifying (5 home wins, unbeaten at home).
- **Team cohesion & spirit** — tight-knit diaspora group, "small country, big heart" identity.
- **Transition speed & set-piece threat** — dangerous on the counter and from dead balls.
- **Experienced spine** — Mendes, Vozinha, Roberto Lopes provide leadership and tournament nous.
- **Momentum** — emphatic 3-0 win over Serbia in the final pre-tournament friendly.

## Weaknesses

- **Squad depth & pedigree gap** — only one top-five-league player; large class gap vs Spain and Uruguay.
- **Logan Costa fitness** — best defender barely match-fit after an ACL injury.
- **Goal-scoring reliance** — heavy dependence on Livramento and aging Mendes for goals.
- **Tournament inexperience** — World Cup debutants; no big-stage track record.
- **Defensive resilience under sustained pressure** — untested against sides of Spain's quality over 90 minutes.

## Verdict

Cape Verde are the romantic underdogs of Group H and will be content simply to compete on the world's biggest stage. Realistically, the gulf to Spain and Uruguay makes progression a long shot, and even the third-place route is contested by a battle-tested Saudi Arabia. But Bubista's disciplined, well-drilled block, genuine set-piece danger and counter-attacking pace mean they are nobody's free points — a draw or an upset, particularly against Saudi Arabia or a complacent opponent, is firmly within reach. Expect them to defend deep, frustrate, and pick their moments. Group exit is the likely outcome; a memorable scalp would crown a historic debut.
